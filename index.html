Research Papers

Regulating Digital Piracy Consumption

Jieteng Chen, Yuetao Gao, T. Tony Ke | Journal of Marketing Research (2024)

This paper develops a model where anti-piracy penalties interact with pirate firms' investment in anti-tracking technology. It shows piracy can rise under stricter penalties if platforms adapt strategically, and identifies when moderate versus strict regulation improves welfare.

Designing Detection Algorithms for AI-Generated Content: Consumer Inference, Creator Incentives, and Platform Strategy

Jieteng Chen, T. Tony Ke, Jiwoong Shin | Revise & Resubmit, Management Science

The paper studies detection-threshold design for AI-generated content labels. It highlights a key tradeoff: stricter detection can reduce false negatives but increase false positives that discourage creators, while looser detection weakens label credibility and trust. The optimal policy balances these forces.

From Canvas to Blockchain: Impact of Royalties on Art Market Efficiency

Xinyu Cao, Jieteng Chen, T. Tony Ke | Minor Revision, Management Science

This project examines resale royalties in art markets. Royalties can reduce resale-market efficiency when popularity is known, but under information asymmetry they may reduce signaling distortions in primary pricing and improve outcomes at moderate rates.
